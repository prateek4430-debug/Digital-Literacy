\# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

\## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a cybercrime where

attackers trick victims into transferring money or revealing

their UPI PIN through deceptive methods.

\## How It Happens (Step by Step)

1\. The attacker contacts the victim via phone or WhatsApp,

posing as a bank official or customer support agent.

2\. They claim there is a problem with the victim's account

or offer a cashback/reward.

3\. The attacker sends a "collect request" on UPI apps like

GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm — which actually withdraws money.

4\. They convince the victim to enter their UPI PIN to

"verify" their account or "receive" the payment.

5\. As soon as the PIN is entered, money is debited from

the victim's account instantly.

\## Who is Targeted?

\- College students new to digital payments

\- Senior citizens unfamiliar with UPI

\- Small business owners

\- People looking for jobs or loans online

\## Consequences

\- Immediate financial loss

\- No easy way to reverse UPI transactions

\- Mental stress and anxiety

\- Risk of identity theft if bank details are shared

\- In some cases, victims lose their entire savings
